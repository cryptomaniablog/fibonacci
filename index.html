One of the literary device used in Harrison Bergeron was verbal irony. Te example of verbal irony I decided to include in this writing would have to be the following passage from Harrison Bergeron. The direct quote is: "The year was 2081, and everybody was finally equal" This is an example of verbal irony. The author describes earlier in the book that equality was being coercered (forced) to be taken up by the people. Despite the statement being a correct statement, the author's true purpose is to sort of "mock" the system that is in place. Although the people are equal it comes at an expensive cost which is the torture which the U.S government uses in the book during the year 2081. A few examples of this would be that the "beautiful" people must conceal their faces through the use of masks of the act of disfiguration of their faces. Intelligent people on the other hand must listen to what would be considered abnormally loud noises which will disallows them from being able to think. And finally the very strong and able must carry weights round their necks all day. Due to these terrible circumstances that the people are under, the authors statement could be considered a debatable topic. This is why we label his statement as verbal irony.

Thanks to the authors use of verbal irony, we can identify a few things: Theme, tone and the authors purpose. Theme: The theme I identified in the story is that for the citizens of the United States at this time will have forced equality to allow for total equality among people. Tone: The tone expressed by the author is a dark tone. This tone is used to spread the author's message, this message is that total equality is not healthy for our society and can be detrimental to the progress of a nation. I touch more upon this in the authors purpose. The author's purpose: The author of Harrison Bergeron is attempting to convey the message that total equality could cause problems in our society. He uses a satire approach to convey his idea, he does this by detailing a dystonian future in which the people of the United States in the year 2081, are subject to awful conditions to keep everyone equal. With the use of satire (exaggeration) he makes the thought of total equality and unthinkable act.
